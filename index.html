The Sacred Significance of Vasavi Jayanti

📅 March 13, 2026✍️ Trust Editorial⏱ 5 min read
🔥

Vasavi Jayanti is one of the most significant observances for the Arya Vysya community — a day that commemorates the extraordinary life and sacrifice of Kanyaka Parameswari, also known as Vasavi Devi.

Who Was Vasavi Devi?

Vasavi Kanyaka Parameswari was the daughter of a wealthy merchant in the Arya Vysya community. Known for her extraordinary beauty, intelligence, and devotion, she became the centre of a dharmic conflict when a powerful king sought to marry her against the wishes of her family and community.

"Rather than compromise the honour of her community and her dharma, Vasavi Devi chose to enter the sacred fire — an act of supreme sacrifice that earned her immortality in the hearts of her people."

How We Observe This Day

At AVSDRS, Vasavi Jayanti is observed with special Poojas at the Vasavi Kanyakaparameshwari Temple in K.R. Puram, followed by Pravachanas, Bhajans, and community gatherings.

Join Us

We warmly invite you to join our observance of Vasavi Jayanti. If you would like to volunteer or contribute, please reach out to us.

Annadana — Why Free Food Distribution is the Highest Form of Seva

📅 March 10, 2026⏱ 4 min read
🍚

In the Sanathana Dharma tradition, there is a saying: "Annadaata Sukhibhava" — may the giver of food be blessed. Annadana has been considered the highest form of charitable service for thousands of years.

The Philosophy Behind Annadana

Food is not merely nourishment for the body — in our tradition, it is a form of Brahman itself. When we offer food to another, we are not simply feeding a person; we are serving the divine within them.

"Anna daanam param daanam — among all forms of charity, the gift of food is supreme."

Our Annadana Programs

AVSDRS is committed to running regular Annadana drives in and around K.R. Puram, Bengaluru. We welcome volunteers and donors to join us in this sacred mission.

Preserving Classical Arts: Our Commitment to Indian Heritage

📅 March 8, 2026⏱ 4 min read
🎶

India's classical arts — Bharatanatyam, Kuchipudi, Carnatic music, Harikatha — are not entertainment. They are a living bridge between the human and the divine.

In an era of rapid cultural change, many classical art forms are under threat. AVSDRS intends to bridge the gap by creating platforms for these arts to reach new audiences.

We plan to organize annual cultural festivals, support young artists through scholarships and performance opportunities. If you are an artist or patron of the arts, we invite you to collaborate with us.

🛕 Religious

Understanding the Bhagavad Gita — Timeless Wisdom for Modern Life

📅 March 5, 2026⏱ 6 min read
📿

The Bhagavad Gita, delivered by Lord Krishna on the battlefield of Kurukshetra, is one of humanity's most profound philosophical texts offering guidance as relevant today as it was five thousand years ago.

The Gita's Core Teaching

At its heart, the Bhagavad Gita teaches us about dharma — our righteous duty — and how to fulfil it without attachment to results.

"Karmanye vadhikaraste ma phaleshu kadachana — you have the right to perform your duty, but not to the fruits of your actions."

Our Gita Study Programs

AVSDRS organises regular Bhagavad Gita pravachanas and study circles where members of all backgrounds can explore these teachings together.

Gau Mata — The Sacred Cow and the Dharma of Cow Protection

📅 Feb 25, 2026⏱ 5 min read
🐄

In Sanathana Dharma, the cow — Gau Mata — occupies a unique and sacred position. She is a symbol of abundance, compassion, and the earth's nurturing power.

Why Gau Seva Matters

Protecting and serving cows is considered one of the highest duties in our tradition.

"Gavo vishwasya matarah — the cow is the mother of the universe."

AVSDRS and Gau Seva

Our Trust supports Gaushalas in and around Bengaluru through financial contributions and volunteer programs. We also organise Gau Puja events and awareness programs.
